Bibimbap

Bibimbap - A traditional Korean dish using a mix of vegetables over rice

One of the first dishes I made with Cynthia when living on Beethoven street. Although the first time it took us almost 3 hours to make. We've refined our process to make it much quicker. We often choose to go heavier on the veggies than listed

Ingredients

  • rice
  • 3 large carrots (Optionally more)
  • 400mg of shittake mushrooms
  • 200mg of spinach or bok choi
  • 1 tbsp avocado or olive oil for frying

    • Cucumber Salad

  • 1 cucumber
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp agave
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp chli flakes

    • Tofu

  • 1 block of tofu
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cloves minced garlic

    • Sauce

  • 2 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp garlic poweder
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Prepare rice of your choice, making sure to wash
  2. Mix sesame oil, soy sauce, and minced garlic in a container
  3. Thinly slice tofu and place in marinade. Shake and place in fridge for 30 minutes
  4. Shred carrots, slice mushrooms, slice leafy greens
  5. Heat frying oil in a pan
  6. Sautee each of the vegetables separate from the others. Season with salt and pepper
  7. Fry tofu, flipping to make sure each side is golden brown
  8. While tofu is frying, mix gochujang, water, sesame oil, sugar, vinegar, garlic powder, and seasame seeds to make sauce
  9. combine all ingredients in a bowl and enjoy
